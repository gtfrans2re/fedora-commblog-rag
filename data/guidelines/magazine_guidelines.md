# Fedora Magazine — Article Guidelines

> Fedora Magazine delivers news and content about the Fedora community to a broad
> audience of Fedora users and Linux enthusiasts — not just contributors. Articles
> should be accessible, technically accurate, and clearly written.

---

## What the Magazine Publishes

- General announcements and news about Fedora
- Tips on how to use software in Fedora
- Ideas for cool apps to try
- Ways to more effectively develop software using Fedora
- DIY hardware projects powered by Fedora
- Everything else related to Fedora in general

> **Important distinction:** Fedora Magazine focuses on Fedora *users*, not just
> contributors. If your article targets contributors specifically, the
> Fedora Community Blog may be a better fit.

---

## Article Length and Scope

- Keep articles focused and concise. If an article exceeds 500-600 words,
  consider breaking it into two or more shorter articles.
- The Magazine also hosts article series, as long as authors are willing to
  write several entries before publishing the first.
- Every article must have a clear target audience, a clear objective, and a
  clear takeaway for the reader.

---

## Writing Style

- Write in **US English** — this is the grammar and spelling standard for the Magazine.
- Write clearly and use shorter sentences. Brevity is good. Clarity is better.
- Avoid passive voice. Prefer active constructions.
  - Active: *The troops defeated the enemy.*
  - Passive: *The enemy was defeated by the troops.*
- Do not be excessively wordy. If a longer sentence is easier to read, use
  the extra words — but avoid padding.
- Use **sentence case** for the post title and all headings. Do not capitalize
  words unless they are proper nouns.
  - Incorrect: *Use Sentence Case for Post Titles in Fedora Magazine*
  - Correct: *Use sentence case for post titles in Fedora Magazine*
- Check spelling and grammar before submitting. Editors will double-check,
  but clean copy makes review faster.

---

## Formatting

- Insert a **Read More** (`<!--more-->`) divider after the first paragraph.
- Use headers (H2, H3) to structure longer articles.
- Use **italics** for GUI/CLI elements mentioned inline in a sentence
  (e.g. button text, menu entries, package names like *dnf install foo*).
- Do not mix monospace fonts with proportional fonts in a sentence — use italics
  for inline mentions instead.
- Use the **Preformatted** style in the WordPress editor for command-line input
  or output.
- Use a shell prompt (`$` or `#`) only where it genuinely affects meaning or
  separates input from output.
- Use **boldface** for the input itself in command examples, and only for
  extremely important phrases elsewhere.
- Use **ordered lists** for sequential steps; **unordered lists** for
  non-sequential items.
- Do not use "here" as link text — bad for accessibility and SEO.

---

## Article Structure (Template)

Follow this structure for how-to and tutorial articles:

1. **Introduction** — One or two sentences explaining when and why a user
   would need this. Do not explain the process yet.
2. **Prerequisites** — Anything the user needs to know or install beforehand.
3. **Steps** — The process itself, broken into clear numbered steps.
4. **Conclusion** — A brief summary of what was accomplished and what the
   reader can do next.

---

## Images

- Every article published on the Magazine **requires a cover image**.
- Cover images must follow the Magazine's cover image guidelines
  (see: https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/fedora-magazine/).
- Provide full attribution for all images per their license.
- Upload all images to WordPress — do not hotlink.
- Do not repeat the featured image in the body of the post.
- Use the article title in the featured image alt tag for an SEO boost.
- For screenshots, use high resolution and crop to show only the relevant area.

---

## Metadata

- **Category:** Select the most appropriate single category.
- **Tags:** Add 3-6 specific, strategic tags. Avoid generic terms.
- **Featured image alt tag:** Use the article title.
- **Yoast SEO:**
  - Set a single focus keyword or phrase.
  - Write a clear article snippet for social sharing.
  - Use the focus keyword in the snippet.

---

## Scheduling

- Fedora Magazine typically publishes on **Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays**.
- Coordinate with the editorial team to avoid conflicts with the Community Blog
  (which targets Tuesdays and Thursdays).
- The editorial board sets the publishing schedule at weekly meetings on
  Matrix: `#magazine:fedoraproject.org` on `chat.fedoraproject.org`.

---

## Editorial Process

1. **Pitch** — Submit an article proposal on the Fedora discussion forum
   (discussion.fedoraproject.org, under the Magazine workflows category).
2. **Draft** — Once the pitch is approved, write the draft in WordPress.
3. **Review** — Editors review the draft and collaborate with the author.
4. **Publish** — Article is scheduled and published.

---

## Editorial Review Checklist

- [ ] Cover image uploaded, attributed, and meets cover image guidelines
- [ ] Read More tag present after the first paragraph
- [ ] All terminal commands tested and accurate
- [ ] Sentence case used for title and all headings
- [ ] No passive voice (where avoidable)
- [ ] No hotlinked images
- [ ] US English spelling and grammar
- [ ] 3-6 tags added
- [ ] Yoast SEO focus keyword and snippet set
- [ ] Article length under 600 words or split into a series
